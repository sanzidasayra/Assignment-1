Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and social entrepreneur, is the pioneer of microfinance and microcredit. In 1976, he founded Grameen Bank, providing small, collateral-free loans to empower impoverished individuals, especially women, to start businesses and break the cycle of poverty. His innovative model transformed traditional banking and inspired global microfinance initiatives. For his efforts to alleviate poverty and promote economic self-reliance, Yunus and Grameen Bank were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006. An advocate of social business, he has authored influential books like Banker to the Poor and remains a global icon for social and economic change.
